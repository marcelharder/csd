Patient Prosthesis Mismatch (PPM)

Patient Prosthesis Mismatch (PPM) occurs when the effective orifice area of a prosthetic heart valve is too small in relation to the required aortic valve area, leading to higher than expected gradients across the valve. The required aortic valve area is determined based on the patient's individual characteristics and expressed in a newly defined algorithm that takes in the gender, the age, the height and the weight of the patient. This condition can result in suboptimal hemodynamic performance and may impact patient outcomes following valve replacement surgery.
