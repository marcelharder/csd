Online Valve Inventory

This application gives a real time overview of the available prosthetic heart valves in your hospital. It allows a cardiac surgeon to plan the operative procedure with the confidence that the intended prosthesis is really available. On the other hand it gives valve vendors a realtime overview of the location of their products. This enables them to intelligently supply the hospitals that they are working with. Cardiohelp

This application records the outcomes of the use of the CardioHelp machine This CardioHelp machine gives extracorporeal support for, amongst others, pulmonary and cardiac conditions. The CardioHelp machine collects a huge amount of hemodynamic, operational and alarm conditions, up to 54 variables every 5 minutes. To manage these data it is very helpful to have a dedicated, cloud based, web application. With these data neatly stored in the cloud we can learn to finetune the indications for extracorporeal support. These data are anonymously collected and therefore there are no patient privacy concerns. Click the button to see a CardioHelp powerpoint presentation:

Patient Prosthesis Mismatch (PPM)

Patient Prosthesis Mismatch (PPM) occurs when the effective orifice area of a prosthetic heart valve is too small in relation to the required aortic valve area, leading to higher than expected gradients across the valve. The required aortic valve area is determined based on the patient's individual characteristics and expressed in a newly defined algorithm that takes in the gender, the age, the height and the weight of the patient. This condition can result in suboptimal hemodynamic performance and may impact patient outcomes following valve replacement surgery. Waitinglist

This application was developed to streamline a referral for cardiac surgery. All the information about the patient is presented to the surgeon. This information includes the angiogram, echo's and other additional investigations that are relevant to the referral. With all the information about a potential surgical case in one place, the surgeon can quickly estimate the surgical risk and envisioned benefit of the surgical procedure. The main benefit of this application is the significant reduction of administrative tasks that accompany a referral for cardiac surgery.
